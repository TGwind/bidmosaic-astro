## Understanding Performance Ad Settlement Models

Performance advertising differs from brand advertising in one core way: advertisers only pay for measurable user actions. Different settlement models correspond to different stages of user behavior, and choosing the right model directly impacts campaign cost and effectiveness.

## Major Settlement Models Explained

### CPI — Cost Per Install

**Definition**: Advertiser pays when a user completes an app installation.

**Best for:**
- App promotion campaigns
- Rapid user base growth
- New app cold-start phases

**Advantages:**
- Predictable costs with pay-per-install pricing
- Suitable for large-scale user acquisition
- Mature tracking infrastructure

**Risks:**
- Install ≠ active user — retention monitoring is essential
- Risk of incentivized installs and low-quality users

### CPA — Cost Per Action

**Definition**: Advertiser pays when a user completes a specified deep action such as registration, first purchase, or checkout.

**Best for:**
- High-quality conversion focus
- Products with clear monetization models
- ROI-strict advertisers

**Advantages:**
- Deeper conversion point means higher user quality
- Directly aligned with business objectives
- Lowest advertiser risk

**Risks:**
- Typically higher bids than CPI
- Available traffic scale may be limited
- Higher attribution accuracy requirements

### CPS — Cost Per Sale

**Definition**: Advertiser pays when a user completes an actual purchase, typically as a fixed percentage of sale value.

**Best for:**
- E-commerce promotion
- Subscription service marketing
- High-AOV products

**Advantages:**
- Directly tied to actual revenue
- Zero risk for advertisers
- Naturally suited to e-commerce

**Risks:**
- Quality publishers may be reluctant (longer settlement cycles)
- Requires robust order postback systems
- Return/refund handling needs clear policies

### CPL — Cost Per Lead

**Definition**: Advertiser pays when a user submits a valid form or lead.

**Best for:**
- B2B customer acquisition
- Education and training industries
- Finance, insurance, and other lead-driven verticals

**Advantages:**
- Direct capture of potential customer information
- Suited to products with long decision cycles
- Predictable costs

**Risks:**
- Lead quality varies widely
- Requires clear "valid lead" definitions
- Downstream conversion rates affect actual acquisition cost

## How to Choose the Optimal Settlement Model

### Decision Framework

1. **Clarify business objectives**: Are you pursuing user volume or deep conversions?
2. **Assess product stage**: CPI for cold-start, CPA/CPS for mature products
3. **Calculate LTV**: Understand user lifetime value to determine reasonable acquisition cost ceilings
4. **Match channel characteristics**: Different channels suit different settlement models

### Hybrid Strategy

In practice, a single settlement model rarely meets all needs. Advertisers can:

- Use CPI to rapidly build a user base
- Use CPA to optimize conversion quality
- Use CPS to lock in actual revenue results
